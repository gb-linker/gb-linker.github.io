Tigers Rally in 11th Inning to Edge Twins; Greene’s Clutch Homer Secures Victory

On August 14, 2025, a tightly contested MLB matchup unfolded at Target Field, home of the Minnesota Twins. The Detroit Tigers secured a dramatic 4-3 walk-off victory over the Twins in the 11th inning, thanks to Gleyber Torres’ sacrifice fly, extending their recent hot streak.

Back-and-Forth Battle: From Deficit to Deadlock

The Twins struck first in the third inning. Edouard Julien ignited the offense with a right-field home run to open the scoring. Alan Martin followed with a double, and Byron Buxton added a single, advancing Martin to third. Ryan Jeffers and Luke Keaschall then delivered RBI hits, fueling a three-run inning that gave Minnesota an early 3-0 lead.

The Tigers refused to fold, mounting a comeback in the fourth inning. Kerry Carpenter kickstarted the rally with a double to right field, and Riley Greene crushed his 28th home run of the season, trimming the deficit to 3-2. In the sixth inning, Colt Keith’s single drove in J. Baez, tying the game at 3-3 and setting the stage for a grueling stalemate.

Crucial Plays: Tense 10th Inning Stand, Walk-Off in 11th

The 10th inning featured high drama. With Minnesota’s Alan Roden on third base, Austin Martin’s half-swing appeared to create a scoring chance, but Tigers first baseman Spencer Torkelson charged aggressively, throwing out Roden at the plate—a call upheld by video review—to preserve the tie.

In the 11th inning, Keith advanced from second to third on Erasmo Ramirez’s wild pitch. Torres then hit a sacrifice fly to right field, allowing Keith to score the winning run, sealing Detroit’s victory.

Statistical Highlights: Key Contributions on Mound and Offense

Detroit’s pitching staff shined, led by starter Tarik Skubal. Despite a rocky third inning, he lasted seven innings, allowing 3 runs with 3 strikeouts, and retired 13 of the final 14 batters he faced to keep the Tigers in contention. Rafael Montero earned the win (1-1) with 1.1 scoreless innings, recording 2 strikeouts.

Offensively, Riley Greene was the standout. His home run drove in 2 runs, and he finished with a .269 batting average and an .828 OPS. J. Baez also contributed, tallying 2 hits and 1 run to keep rallies alive.

For the Twins, Edouard Julien’s home run was a bright spot—he went 2-for-3 with 1 RBI, boosting his season average to .198. However, the bullpen faltered in extra innings, with Erasmo Ramirez (0-1) taking the loss.

Season Trajectory: Tigers Regain Momentum to Solidify Lead

The win improved Detroit’s record to 71-52. The Tigers have won 11 of their last 17 games, bouncing back from a mid-July slump (12 losses in 13 games) to reaffirm their division lead. Meanwhile, Minnesota dropped to 57-64, falling further out of playoff contention.

The two teams will face off again on Friday, with Detroit sending Charlie Morton (7-10, 5.48 ERA) to the mound against Minnesota’s Pierson Ohl (0-2, 7.15 ERA). The series’ next chapter promises to be compelling.
