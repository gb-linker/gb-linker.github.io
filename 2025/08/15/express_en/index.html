Guides Related to International Express Mailing

1. List of Prohibited Items for International Express

  • Dangerous goods: Including inflammable and explosive items (such as firecrackers, gasoline, alcohol), corrosive items (such as sulfuric acid, hydrochloric acid), radioactive items, compressed gases (such as lighter gas, oxygen cylinders), etc. These items are prone to causing safety accidents during transportation and are strictly prohibited from being mailed.

  • Contraband items: Covering drugs, firearms and ammunition, controlled knives, pornographic items, counterfeit currencies, etc., which are explicitly prohibited from circulation and transportation by law.

  • Restricted items: Some countries have restrictive regulations on food (especially meat, fruits), medicines, animal and plant products, cultural and artistic relics, etc. Before mailing, it is necessary to check the specific requirements of the destination country to avoid items being detained or destroyed due to violations.

2. International Express Packaging Guidelines

  • Ordinary items: Choose a sturdy carton. After putting the items in, fill them with cushioning materials (such as bubble wrap, foam particles) to ensure that the items do not shake. Reinforce the outside of the carton with tape in a criss - cross pattern to prevent cracking during transportation.

  • Fragile items: For glass products, ceramics, etc., first wrap them with foam pads or bubble wrap for multiple layers, then put them into customized foam molds or cartons. Fill the gaps with soft fillers, and mark the “fragile” sign on the outside of the box.

  • Liquid items: Seal them in leak - proof containers. The capacity of each container should not be too large. Wrap the outside with a plastic bag and tie it tightly, then put it into a carton. Wrap the surrounding area with hygroscopic materials to prevent leakage from polluting other items.

  • Valuable items: Use anti - pressure and anti - shock special packaging, and can be equipped with a wooden frame. At the same time, mark “valuable items” on the outside of the packaging, and it is recommended to insure them.

3. Essential Knowledge for International Express Customs Clearance

  • Required documents: Generally including commercial invoices (which need to indicate the name, quantity, value, purpose of the items, etc.), packing lists (detailed listing of the items in the package), and some items also need to provide certificates of origin, health quarantine certificates, etc.

  • Declaration requirements: truthfully declare item information, including name, quantity, value, etc. The deviation between the declared value and the actual value should not be too large, otherwise, it may be fined or detained by the customs.

  • Customs clearance process: After the items arrive in the destination country, the customs will inspect the items, check whether the declared information is consistent with the actual items. If the inspection is passed, they will be released; otherwise, they may be required to supplement documents, or conduct taxation, return, etc.

  • Characteristics of customs clearance in various countries: The efficiency and strictness of customs clearance vary from country to country. For example, customs clearance in the United States is relatively efficient, but the supervision of food and drugs is stricter; customs clearance procedures in Brazil are complex and prone to delays, so preparations should be made in advance.

4. Calculation Method of International Express Fees

  • Weight calculation: Divided into actual weight and volumetric weight. Volumetric weight = length × width × height (cm) ÷ 5000 (some express companies calculate by 6000). The larger of the two is taken as the charging weight.

  • Basic fee: Determined according to the charging weight and the destination country/region. Different express companies have different rates. Generally, the farther the distance and the larger the weight, the higher the fee.

  • Surcharges: Including fuel surcharge (fluctuating with fuel prices), remote area surcharge (additional payment for shipping to remote areas), overweight and oversize surcharge (items whose weight or size exceeds the specified standards), etc.

  • Other fees: Such as insurance fee (charged according to a certain proportion of the insured amount), customs clearance agency service fee (if it is necessary to entrust an express company for customs clearance), etc.

5. Practical Tips for International Express Mailing

  • Choose a suitable express company: Choose according to the nature of the items, destination, budget and time - sensitive requirements. For example, DHL is fast but expensive, suitable for urgent items; EMS has strong customs clearance ability, suitable for sensitive items.

  • Pack reasonably to reduce weight: Remove unnecessary packaging of items, compress the volume, reduce the charging weight, and lower the cost.

  • Understand the policies in advance: Check the import tax rate and prohibited regulations of the destination country to avoid delays or detention of items due to policy issues.

  • Keep relevant vouchers: Properly keep the express waybill number, invoice, declaration documents, etc., so as to track the express information and handle subsequent problems.

  • Avoid peak periods: Before and after holidays are the peak periods for international express, and the time efficiency may be delayed, so try to mail in off - peak periods.

6. Guidelines for Mailing Special Items via International Express

  • Electronic products: It is necessary to ensure that the items are new and unopened. Some countries require 3C certification, invoices and other documents. Batteries need to be packed separately, in line with international air transport standards, and lithium batteries have capacity restrictions.

  • Cosmetics: For liquid cosmetics, attention should be paid to the capacity. Generally, each bottle should not exceed 100ml, and it should be tightly sealed. Cosmetics containing alcohol may be restricted by some airlines.

  • Medicines: Prescription drugs need to provide a prescription certificate issued by a doctor, and over - the - counter drugs need to explain their ingredients and uses. Some countries have strict restrictions on traditional Chinese medicine, so it is necessary to confirm in advance.

  • Luxury goods: High - value luxury goods need to truthfully declare their value, may need to pay higher tariffs, and it is recommended to choose the insurance service.

7. Notes on International Express Mailing to Major Countries

  • The United States: Mailing of meat, fruits and other foods is prohibited. Medicines need to provide English instructions and doctor’s prescriptions. The import tariff threshold is 800 US dollars, and items below this amount may be duty - free.

  • EU countries: Electronic products, cosmetics, etc. need to meet the CE certification standards. The value - added tax rate is relatively high, and it is necessary to pay according to regulations during customs clearance.

  • Japan: Mailing of fresh fruits and vegetables is prohibited. Medicines need to provide detailed ingredient instructions. The express delivery time is relatively fast, usually 3 - 5 days.

  • Australia: The supervision of animal and plant products and food is strict, and unpacking inspection may be carried out. Mailing of food containing meat is prohibited.

  • Middle Eastern countries: Sensitive to religious - related items, avoid mailing pornographic and religiously extreme items. Some countries have special regulations on women’s products.

8. Handling Process for Loss/Damage of International Express

  • Contact the express company in time: After finding that the express is lost or damaged, call the customer service number of the express company as soon as possible, explain the situation and provide the express waybill number, item information, etc.

  • Submit relevant certificates: According to the requirements of the express company, provide the purchase invoice of the item, photos (proving the damage of the item), packaging conditions and other supporting materials.

  • Negotiate compensation: The express company will determine the loss according to the value of the item, insurance situation, etc., and the two parties will negotiate the compensation amount. For insured items, compensation is generally made according to the insured amount; for uninsured items, the compensation amount is usually limited.

  • Appeal and rights protection: If you are not satisfied with the handling result of the express company, you can complain to the relevant regulatory authorities, such as the State Post Bureau.

9. Factors Affecting the Timeliness of International Express

  • Distance and transportation mode: The farther the destination, the longer the time. Air transportation is fast, sea transportation is slow, and land transportation is in between.

  • Customs clearance efficiency: Customs clearance is a key factor affecting the timeliness. If the declared information of the items is inaccurate or the documents are incomplete, it may lead to customs clearance delays.

  • Weather and natural disasters: Bad weather (such as typhoons, blizzards) and natural disasters (such as earthquakes, tsunamis) may affect the normal operation of transportation tools, resulting in delayed timeliness.

  • Holidays and peak periods: During major domestic and foreign holidays, the volume of express business surges, the processing efficiency decreases, and the timeliness may be extended.

  • Remote areas: Express delivery to remote areas needs to go through multiple transfers, and the timeliness is relatively long.

10. Suggestions for Purchasing International Express Insurance

  • Necessity of purchase: It is recommended to purchase insurance for high - value items, fragile items and valuable items to reduce the risk of loss or damage during transportation.

  • Insurance premium: Generally, it is charged according to a certain proportion of the declared value of the item, usually between 1% - 5%, and the rate varies among different express companies.

  • Insurance coverage: Clarify the situations covered by the insurance, such as loss, total loss, partial damage, etc., and understand the exclusion clauses (such as damage caused by the item’s own quality problems may not be compensated).

  • Insurance application process: When mailing the express, apply for insurance to the express company, fill in the item value and other information, pay the insurance premium, and obtain the insurance certificate.

  • Notes on claim settlement: After an insurance accident occurs, it is necessary to notify the insurance company in time, provide relevant supporting materials, and go through the claim settlement procedures according to the requirements of the insurance company.
